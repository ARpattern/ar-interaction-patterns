Welcome to AR Interaction Patterns — A Research Showcase on HMD-Based Interaction Design in Augmented Reality

This website showcases the PhD research of Yu Liu at Hochschule RheinMain, focusing on the design and validation of interaction patterns for Head-Mounted Display (HMD)-based Augmented Reality (AR) experiences. It aims to support the development of intuitive, effective AR interactions—especially within cultural heritage contexts.

Platform Structure

The platform is structured around two main components:

  • Design Patterns: A collection of rigorously developed HMD-based AR interaction patterns, presented through scenario descriptions, interaction flows, and an Event–Condition–Action (ECA) model.
  • Technology Module (under development): A library of reusable Unity-based prefabs that implement these patterns, to be published on GitHub for direct integration.

Research Context

This work is embedded in two European projects: presentXR and LoGa Culture, both exploring innovative AR interaction paradigms for museums and cultural institutions.

By bridging design theory and technical implementation, this project advances pattern-based methodologies in immersive media and invites collaboration and feedback from the global AR community.
